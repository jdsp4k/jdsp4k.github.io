Research Papers

Multispectral Projections: Using Spectral Super-Resolution (SSR) to Generate Multispectral Visualizations of Manuscripts for the Recovery of Lost and Damaged Text
Pending Publication

Our team, funded by the National Endowment for the Humanities, developed a deep-learning Spectral Super-Resolution (SSR) model to infer multispectral data from standard RGB images. This cost-effective method allows preliminary analysis of manuscript leaves and illustrations to identify elements that traditional multispectral imaging would reveal. Our paper details the SSR model's development and demonstrates its effectiveness using an open-source dataset to uncover obscured writing on the Lichfield Gospels manuscript.
